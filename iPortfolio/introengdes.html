4.4 Miniature Train

For this project, my team aimed to create an interactive model for young children, adhering to a uniform theme and specific constraints from a design brief. Here's an overview of our approach and my contributions:

Objective and Theme:

  • Designed a model to be interactive and engaging for young children.
  • Chose a dragon theme, leveraging its popularity in children's shows.
  • Incorporated creative elements such as legs instead of traditional wheels to mimic a dragon's movement.

My Role and Contributions:

  • Divided the dragon into sections, focusing on the middle portion of the model.
  • Designed the dragon's body with an inorganic form and slots for wing attachment.
  • Applied a color gradient with scales to enhance the model's visual appeal.

Skills and Tools:

  • Utilized Autodesk Inventor for the design process.
  • Employed advanced techniques such as sweeping and drive constraints to maximize the model's potential.

Our project successfully blended creativity with technical precision, resulting in an engaging and visually appealing model for children.

P125 Mechanical Winch

October 14, 2019 - October 23, 2019

Objective: Model a prototype winch using vex parts, and create a NetLogo simulation of the prototype.

For this project, my team was required to design a mechanical winch that would not only handle heavy weights but perform this with speed. We also needed to document our progress, and create a NetLogo simulation. Here’s an overview of our approach and my contributions:

Objective and Theme:

  • Designed a mechanical winch to handle heavy weights with speed.
  • Documented the progress and created a NetLogo simulation of the prototype.
  • Utilized Vex parts and a Fishertek motor with a maximum of 7.5 volts.

My Role and Contributions:

  • Assigned tasks for the team and created the design for the winch.
  • Built the winch, using two pulleys for larger weight support and a large gear for higher torque.
  • Collaborated with the main programmer to create a NetLogo simulation with adjustable voltage and weight features.

Skills and Tools:

  • Applied design principles of sprocket and chain, and pulleys for more torque.
  • Used engineering principles and calculations to solve issues related to friction, motor and gear misalignment, and spacing the sprocket and chain system.

Our project successfully combined technical expertise with practical application, resulting in a functional and efficient mechanical winch.

Renewable Resources Vehicle

Objective: Create a vehicle powered by a variety of renewable resources using Vex and Fishertek parts, and a Vex V4 motor.

Creating a vehicle that would move based on a variety of renewable resources was the objective for this project. My team and I were required to build this vehicle out of Vex and Fishertek parts, and breadboard a renewable power source to a Vex V4 motor. Here’s an overview of our approach and my contributions:

Objective and Theme:

  • Designed a vehicle powered by renewable resources.
  • Built the vehicle using Vex and Fishertek parts, adhering to project constraints.
  • Breadboarded a renewable power source to a Vex V4 motor.

My Role and Contributions:

  • Assigned tasks for the team and created designs for the vehicle.
  • Built the vehicle, incorporating a directly powered back wheel connected to the smaller front wheel through tread to increase torque.
  • Documented the entire process, including multiple design iterations.

Skills and Tools:

  • Applied design principles to ensure the vehicle met project constraints and was lightweight enough for the power sources.
  • Utilized Vex and Fishertek parts effectively to create a functional design.
  • Developed renewable power solutions using solar panels and hydrogen fuel cells.

Challenges and Solutions:

  • Addressed power source limitations by experimenting with solar panels and hydrogen fuel cells, despite their limited power.
  • Overcame size constraints by optimizing the design to fit within the 5-inch width limit, using 3 inches for the two-wheel configuration.
  • Resolved design and weight issues through careful calculations and iterative improvements.

Our project successfully combined technical expertise, renewable energy solutions, and innovative design, resulting in a functional and efficient vehicle powered by renewable resources.
