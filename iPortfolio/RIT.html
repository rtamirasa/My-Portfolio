APP Presentation

June 7, 2020 - June 19, 2020

Objective: Present the molecular breakdown of a gene related to the brain.

For this project, I worked with a partner to present the molecular breakdown of the amyloid precursor protein (APP). Here’s an overview of our approach and contributions:

Objective and Theme:

  • Presented the molecular breakdown of the APP gene related to the brain.
  • Used tools such as STRING, a Manhattan plot, and JMOL for visualization and analysis.
  • Explained the molecular components and significance of the APP gene.

My Role and Contributions:

  • Collaborated with a partner to divide the project into five sections: Alzheimer's disease, the gene, current research, our hypothesized research, and a conclusion.
  • Used JMOL to model the protein and STRING to identify relationships between beta amyloid cleavage and genes.
  • Analyzed data using a Manhattan plot to determine the statistical significance of different gene mutations.

Skills and Tools:

  • Utilized JMOL for protein modeling and visualization.
  • Employed STRING to find gene relationships and interactions.
  • Analyzed genetic data with a Manhattan plot to assess statistical significance.
  • Enhanced data analysis and understanding of complex research topics.

Challenges and Solutions:

  • Faced challenges in understanding and explaining complex molecular data.
  • Overcame these challenges through collaboration and regular calls outside class hours.
  • Used new software tools and conducted remote protein modeling to ensure a comprehensive presentation.

Our project successfully combined scientific research, data analysis, and collaboration to provide a detailed explanation of the APP gene and its significance in Alzheimer's disease.

P125 555 Timer

December 8, 2021 - December 19, 2021

Objective: Model a prototype 555 timer using Multisim circuit diagrams and a school-issued electronic kit.

For this project, I was required to design a circuit 555 timer first on the National Instruments Multisim and then with an electronic kit. Here’s an overview of my approach and contributions:

Objective and Theme:

  • Designed a 555 timer circuit using Multisim and an electronic kit.
  • Documented progress in an engineering notebook.
  • Ensured the circuit allowed 4 LEDs to "blink" at consecutive intervals.

My Role and Contributions:

  • Placed all required components in the Multisim diagram.
  • Wired each integrated circuit channel to ensure proper circuit flow.
  • Used the diagram to connect the ICs similarly with the electronic kit.

Skills and Tools:

  • Utilized National Instruments Multisim for circuit design.
  • Applied knowledge of integrated capacitors and their pin diagrams.
  • Documented the entire process in an engineering notebook.

Challenges and Solutions:

  • Faced difficulties in wiring the circuit properly and dealing with part malfunctions.
  • Resolved issues by verifying wiring accuracy and transferring wiring to a new breadboard when the initial one was found to be broken.

Our project successfully combined theoretical knowledge with practical application, resulting in a functional 555 timer circuit with properly blinking LEDs.
